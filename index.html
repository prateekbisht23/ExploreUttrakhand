About Uttarakhand

Discover the divine beauty and rich heritage of Devbhoomi Uttarakhand, where spirituality meets adventure in the lap of the Himalayas.

The Land of Gods

Uttarakhand, also known as Devbhoomi or the Land of Gods, is a state in northern India that's known for its natural beauty, pilgrimage sites, and adventure tourism opportunities. The state is divided into two regions – Garhwal and Kumaon – each with its distinct cultural heritage.

Rich Biodiversity

Home to diverse flora and fauna, Uttarakhand hosts several national parks and wildlife sanctuaries. The state's varying altitude from 300 to 7,800 meters above sea level creates different ecological zones, supporting unique biodiversity.

Cultural Heritage

The state preserves its rich cultural heritage through traditional art forms, festivals, and customs. The local architecture, handicrafts, and folk music reflect the deep-rooted traditions of the region.

Featured Destinations

From spiritual retreats to adventure hotspots, discover the diverse attractions of Uttarakhand.

Kedarnath Temple

One of the holiest Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, located amidst the magnificent Himalayas.

Valley of Flowers

A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its meadows of endemic alpine flowers and diverse flora.

Rishikesh

The Yoga Capital of the World, where spirituality meets adventure on the banks of the holy Ganges.

Nainital

A charming hill station built around a natural lake, perfect for boating and scenic mountain views.

Auli

India's premier ski resort destination with breathtaking views of Nanda Devi and other Himalayan peaks.

Jim Corbett National Park

India's oldest national park, famous for its Bengal tigers and diverse wildlife experiences.
